Billiards Tricks

Quick Breakdown

Billiards is a cue sport played on a rectangular table covered with a cloth (usually green) and bounded by rubber cushions. It involves using a cue stick to strike balls and achieve specific goals depending on the game variation.

How to Execute the Masse Shot

One of the most impressive tricks in billiards is the Masse Shot—a curved shot that lets you swerve the cue ball around obstacles. It takes serious skill, but it's super satisfying to pull off.

  1. Grip: Hold the cue vertically, almost like you're stabbing downward.
  2. Position: Aim to hit the cue ball off-center with extreme spin (English).
  3. Power: Strike with a firm, controlled force to generate curve.
  4. Curve: Watch as the ball bends around your target and sinks the shot!

History of Billiards Tricks

Billiards has evolved from a royal pastime into a sport of skill and flair. Trick shots became popular in exhibitions during the 19th century, showcasing the creative side of the game.

Originally played on grass before moving indoors, billiards developed into various forms—snooker, carom, pool—each with its own trick shot legends. Players like Willie Hoppe and Efren Reyes have turned shots into art.

Today, trick shots are a major part of competitions and social media clips, keeping the game alive and flashy for new generations.
